In a wide valley stood a great dam built by a colony of beavers. 
Day after day, they repaired leaks, strengthened walls, cleared debris, and extended canals so that water reached every corner of the forest.

The work was never glamorous.
When storms came, the beavers worked through the night. 
When branches broke loose, they removed them. When new families settled nearby, the beavers dug fresh channels so everyone could benefit from the water.

The Council of Owls, perched high above, constantly issued plans.
"This year, reinforce the eastern wall."
The beavers obeyed.
"Next season, improve the southern canal."
The beavers obeyed again.
Every season brought more plans, and every season the beavers carried them out.

![](/images/blog/beaver_dam1.webp)

Among the colony, however, lived a few magpies.
Unlike the beavers, the magpies rarely spent long repairing dams or digging canals. 
They did enough to avoid criticism, but their attention was always drawn elsewhere.
Whenever an owl casually mentioned a curious idea...
"Wouldn't it be interesting if the water sparkled at night?"
or
"I wonder whether fish might prefer canals shaped like spirals?"
the magpies rushed away.

Within days they returned carrying shiny pebbles, colorful shells, or strange decorations. 
They attached them to parts of the dam and proudly presented them to the Council.
The owls were delighted.
"Remarkable initiative!" they cried.
The beavers continued repairing flood damage.
The magpies received praise.

![](/images/blog/beaver_dam2.png)

The next season, the Council issued even more plans.
"Expand the northern canal."
"Strengthen the foundations."
"Support the growing rabbit villages."
The beavers set to work.
Meanwhile, during a meeting, one owl casually remarked,
"It would be fascinating if the dam had a waterfall that sang in the wind."
The magpies vanished.

Weeks later they returned with reeds, shells, and polished stones arranged into a musical contraption.
The owls applauded loudly.
"What creativity!"
"What vision!"
"What leadership!"
Several magpies were promoted to positions overseeing the colony.

The beavers looked at one another in confusion.
It was, after all, the owls who had ordered the canals.
It was the owls who had demanded the repairs.
It was the owls who had created the endless list of necessary work.
Yet the praise never went to those who completed the plans.

Instead, it went to those who abandoned them whenever a passing fancy appeared.
Years passed.
The dam remained standing because the beavers maintained it.
The canals continued flowing because the beavers expanded them.
The villages prospered because the beavers supported them.

Meanwhile, the promoted magpies spent more and more time searching for shiny new pebbles.
Eventually many of them left for distant valleys.
There, they found new councils of owls, new passing remarks, and new opportunities to decorate unfinished dams.

![](/images/blog/beaver_dam3.png)

The beavers remained behind, repairing the structures that everyone depended upon.
One young beaver finally asked an elder:
"Why do the owls reward the magpies more than the builders?"
The elder smiled sadly.
"Because a repaired wall prevents a flood that never happens."
"And a canal extension simply delivers water as expected."
"But a shiny pebble catches the eye."

The young beaver considered this.
"Then why do the owls create plans if they admire the things outside the plans?"
The elder looked toward the council tree.
"That, my child, is a question even the owls have never answered."

And as another leak appeared in the dam, the beavers returned to work while the owls gathered to discuss a fascinating new idea involving floating lanterns.

Moral: Many leaders claim to value execution, yet often reward those who chase visible novelties over those who accomplish the work the leaders themselves declared important.
